USB-C Receptacles

The receptacle features four power and four ground pins, two differential pairs (connected together on devices) for legacy USB 2.0 high-speed data, four shielded differential pairs for Enhanced SuperSpeed data (two transmit and two receive pairs), two Sideband Use (SBU) pins, and two Configuration Channel (CC) pins.

The USB-C receptacle is designed to accept the USB-C plug in either orientation, thanks to its symmetrical design. This means that users do not have to worry about plugging in the cable upside down, making it more convenient and user-friendly.

Pin Name Description
A1 GND Ground Return
A2 TX1+ Transmit data (positive)
A3 TX1- Transmit data (negative)
A4 Vbus Power Supply
A5 CC1 Configuration Channel 1
A6 D+ USB 2.0 Data (positive)
A7 D- USB 2.0 Data (negative)
A8 SBU1 Sideband Use 1
A9 Vbus Power Supply
A10 RX2- Receive data (negative)
A11 RX2+ Receive data (positive)
A12 GND Ground Return
The B1-B12 pins mirror the A1-A12 pins, allowing for the reversible nature of the USB-C connector.

The CC pins are used for cable orientation detection, power delivery negotiation, and role detection (host or device). The SBU pins are reserved for future use and can be utilized for alternate modes, such as audio or video transmission.
